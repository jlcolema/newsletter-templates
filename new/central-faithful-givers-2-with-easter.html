CROSSPOINT FAITHFUL GIVERS

April 7, 2020

Dear Crosspoint Faithful Giver,

Who would have ever thought that we would be where we are today? Well, where are we? We are in a 30-day lockdown right now until April 30, with the possibility of it going even further. To say we are in unchartered territory is an understatement. We are in a once in a century health crisis that no one living has seen. The unemployed zoomed upward by 10 million in two weeks. Enough of all the bad news, I am sure that you know it by heart!

The Good News is that Jesus is Lord! He was crucified, dead and buried. And on the third day He arose from the dead!! Happy Resurrection Sunday. Happy Easter. This is the Good News of the gospel: that God sent His Son, Jesus, to do for us what we could not do for ourselves. He came to pay the penalty for our sins (Good Friday) and then verified that He could do that by raising Himself from the dead (Easter Sunday Morning)! In light of everything going on, this still remains true, and it supersedes anything that is going on in our world. The Resurrection changes everything!

But wait, there is more good news. Crosspoint is remaining strong and we are mobilizing in significant ways to care for our own congregation and to reach out to our communities. I want to take this opportunity to thank all of you that responded to my last letter asking for above and beyond giving or accelerated giving. Through your significant gifts, we have been able to be at +$75,000 over our prorated budgetary needs at the end of March. In addition, we have reduced our expenditures significantly, too. This is helping to make a difference in a time that we are unable to meet and receive offerings through the offering plate.

But wait, there is more good news. Crosspoint has provided meals for an entire ER staff and made "appreciation bags" to a team of medical staff at Eglin. On Friday, April 10, we will host a Grocery Giveaway at the Community Life Center from 10:00 a.m. until 12:30 p.m. and again on Friday, April 24. We are calling and sending cards to hundreds of people. Our new Online Campus worship services are touching both traditional and contemporary worshipers. New people are being reached. Online benevolence is available for those who demonstrate need. Even Discover Crosspoint is going online. 30 new people joined the Prayer Ministry last week. Our staff and all of their volunteer teams are hard at work doing the ministry, just in new and different ways!

I just wanted to write a note and let you know how appreciated you are! Thank you for loving the Lord and being available to Him in times like these! Thank you for keeping Crosspoint strong and keeping us viable in ministry!

In Christ,

Rurel Ausley, Lead Pastor
