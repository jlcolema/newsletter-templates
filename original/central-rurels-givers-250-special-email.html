CROSSPOINT FAITHFUL GIVERS

March 18, 2020

Dear Crosspoint Faithful Giver,

We all are finding ourselves in new territory with this novel COVID-19 (Coronavirus). Crosspoint's leadership is continually monitoring and evaluating our path forward. As God's church we are tasked to connect people to Christ, yet we want to honor our governmental authorities, Center for Disease Control, and our Bishop, so that we are not exposing our people or community to unnecessary risk.

We also want to be an example of God's faith, hope and love in our community by keeping our life giving ministries to our kids, students, and adults going as creatively as we can. We are exploring all possibilities. Our doors are staying open on a daily basis for ministry, planning and help.

This is why I wanted to write a note to you, one of our most faithful givers. First, just to say thank you for your faithful generosity! Without you, we would not be the church that we are! Obviously, it takes us all for Crosspoint to work but your role is a very vital role in giving. Moreover, it will take people like you to get us through this crisis.

As of now, we have no idea how long we may not be meeting in person. Our Bishop has asked us not to meet through the rest of March. As you and I know, the delay keeps being extended, as this crisis has not yet peaked.

Thus, my request of you, is to ask the Lord if there anything you can do to help right now. If you're facing a loss of income yourself, please do not worry about this request. If you have the capacity, I ask you to consider several things. Accelerate your normal giving now or make above and beyond normal contributions to help make up for the significant loss of income that Crosspoint expects to have. I think this action will be critical.

This is not going to be a short-term issue. Even if it is short term, the economy will need to recover and many will not be able to honor their commitments. This will not be something that just one of us can fix. It is going to take a team of us. Lisa and I will be looking for ways to increase our financial gifts during these days, as I am asking you to do the same. I have seen this church do tremendous financial things in my 22 years. Many of you have seen more. Some of us can do some things. With God, all of us can do amazing things!

Thank you for considering this responsibility to keep Crosspoint fully up and in ministry. I know the Lord has something special for Crosspoint to do in this community. I know the Lord has something special for each one of us to do, too. Part of that, today, might be financial, as our capacity allows.

In Christ,
