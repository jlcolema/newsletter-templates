CORONAVIRUS STATEMENT

March 11, 2020

Dear Members and Friends of Crosspoint,

With all the focus on COVID-19 (Coronavirus), we want to assure you that Crosspoint is committed to the health and safety of everyone connected with the church and its ministries.

To date there have been no reported cases of Coronavirus within the Crosspoint community. Since it's likely there will eventually be members or friends of Crosspoint affected by the virus, I wanted to share some thoughts:

What we at Crosspoint will do:

  • Crosspoint is working on a plan and is staying up-to-date on the Coronavirus situation. If a crisis develops, the church will be here for its members and the community.
  • Church-related activities may be reduced or canceled if the situation warrants (group meetings, church dinners, committee meetings, Bargain Box, extra-curricular activities, etc.). Any changes will be communicated by email and through our website and social media posts.
  • If necessary, any decision to close one or more campuses will be made by our Leadership Team and campus pastor affected. If a campus is closed, we will communicate this by similar means.
  • Likewise, the decision to close the Crosspoint Academy will be made by our Leadership Team and our Academy Director. We will communicate this with affected parents and by similar methods.
  • Pastoral care to homes and hospitals may be modified (phone calls and cards, minimal personal contact, etc.) as the situation warrants.
  • Watch for changes in how we greet each other in worship and how we take communion. Any changes will be communicated during our services.

What you can do:

  • Pray for a quick resolution to the COVID-19C outbreak. Ask for God's healing and protective presence as we navigate the challenges presented by Coronavirus. Pray for those affected by the virus.
  • Wash your hands often and use hand sanitizer. Avoid touching your face.
  • Sanitize commonly-handled devices and surfaces--phone, keyboard, purse, countertop, steering wheel, etc.
  • When meeting other people, avoid shaking hands or hugging--greet each other in alternative fashions: smile and wave; fist bump; elbow bump; place your hand on your heart.
  • If you are feeling sick or your health places you in a vulnerable condition, stay home to reduce risk of spreading your sickness or getting sick. When in doubt, stay home and stay safe. You can join us in worship at our online campus: https://crosspoint.church/online/
  • Check the Crosspoint website and social media often for updates or changes.
  • Follow Center for Disease Control (CDC) and county health department guidance: CDC: https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/index.html) Okaloosa County Health Dept: http://okaloosa.floridahealth.gov/

The bottom line is that we intend to continue church life and services as much as possible but we will respond as necessary to address any effects of COVID-19. Please continue to pray for those already affected, and as always, for the church and its leadership.

Yours in Christ,
