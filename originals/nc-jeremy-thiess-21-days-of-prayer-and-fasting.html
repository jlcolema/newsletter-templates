Today we will pray for Crosspoint's Pastors and staff as they take time to be refreshed at their annual staff retreat.

Let’s read together from the prophet Jeremiah.

"And I will give you shepherds after my own heart, who will guide you with knowledge and understanding." Jeremiah 3:15 (NLT)

Jesus is our Good Shepherd (John 10:11). We're thankful for our Shepherd's protection, guidance and nurturing as His 'flock,' and we're thankful that Jesus calls gifted men and women 'shepherds' (also known as 'Pastors') to likewise watch over and encourage His Church. Pastors give of themselves sacrificially with their time, energy, and resources to teach, counsel, and serve those in need; all while carrying an expectation from Scripture to live at a higher standard than both the church and world around them (James 3:1).

Pastors can sometimes seem like 'supermen' or 'superwomen,' however they are people just like us. They not only battle the same trials and temptations we all face, but due to their position of spiritual influence, they live with a constant target on their back under attack from an enemy who desires to destroy the work of the Gospel. The battles they fight is not against flesh and blood but against the spiritual forces of evil (Ephesians 6:10-12).

So let's be diligent to cover our Pastors and church staff with prayer as they spiritual 'fight' to guide us closer to God's heart with knowledge and understanding of God's Word.

Please be praying for:

  •  Lead Pastor, Rurel Ausley, and Crosspoint's Directional Leadership Team to be filled with God's vision, words and wisdom and as they lead Crosspoint to connect people to Christ in communities across northwest Florida.
  • Pastors and church staff to be fueled with a passion to connect people to Christ that overflows from their own daily personal connection to Christ.
  • Regular periods of rest and refreshment (Sabbath) for our Pastors and church staff to prevent burnout. 
  • Protection, peace, and health for our Pastors and their family members.
  • Good friends to surround our Pastors who can encourage them and hold them accountable.
